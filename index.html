the real point of the week

A week for finding purpose

Ages 10 to 13 are when kids quietly decide what they're "good at" and what they're not. The most valuable thing we can give them at that age isn't another skill — it's the discovery that something they love can matter to other people. That's what this week is for. Your kid picks the thing they already care about, turns it into a project that's truly theirs, and watches real people respond to it. Confidence stops being theoretical, because they present to adults in English. Focus comes naturally, because the project is theirs from first idea to finished build. And AI becomes a creation tool in their hands — most kids (and parents) are amazed by how much they can actually make in five days. It's a week of summer, yes. It's also the week your kid learns what it feels like to be about something.

the plan for the week

From "I love this" to "I built this" in 5 days

Hands-on from minute one. Kids build a passion project — football stats, gaming, collecting, whatever they love — and every skill shows up exactly when their project needs it: interviews, design, product photos, posters and a podcast episode all happen because their idea demands them. And because every group of kids is different, we adapt the schedule to their pace, energy and projects.

Mon · 27

Ideas

What do you actually care about? Founders go solo or team up, pick a passion project — a fantasy-league helper, a page for their gaming group, a sneaker or card price tracker — and use AI to test and improve it. First page goes live before snack time.

Tue · 28

Real feedback

Each team writes its own questions and interviews real people. Every answer makes the idea sharper and stronger.

Wed · 29

Build + shoot

Make the product. Then a photo session with professional cameras and a Canva design session: logo, posters, social posts. The project page comes alive.

Thu · 30

Story studio

Record a podcast episode with video in our studio to tell the project's story. Edit, publish, share. Practise the big talk for Showcase Day.

Fri · 31

Showcase Day

Families invited. Each founder presents what they built, the story behind it and what they'd do next. Family and friends try what the kids made and see what a week of building adds up to.

SHOW TIME!

every founder leaves with: a real product · a project page with their own photos · a podcast episode of their own · a certificate. Things they'll proudly show the grandparents!

what you'll notice at home

Skills school doesn't teach yet

Here's what parents should expect to see in their kid the week after: new behaviours, not just new knowledge. All sessions taught in English, with native trainers in English, Portuguese and French on the floor.

🧭

A direction of their own

They picked the thing they love, owned every decision from first idea to finished build, and found out it can matter to other people. That sense of purpose, and the follow-through, comes home with them.

🤖

Uses AI and screens to create, not just consume

They spent a week directing AI to build their idea, a stat tracker, a card-swap matcher, instead of scrolling someone else's feed. They learn to be the boss of the tool, and discover they can make far more with it than they ever imagined.

🎤

Comfortable talking to, and learning from, adults and strangers

They asked real people real questions, listened carefully, and improved their idea based on facts instead of feelings. Watch them start asking better questions everywhere.

Product photos shot on pro cameras, designs made in Canva, a podcast episode recorded in a real studio. Tangible, shareable proof of what they can do.

💶

Understands what things cost, and why

They worked out what their project needed and what it would cost to make. Money stops being abstract, and it's math they wanted to do.

🗣️

Stands up and speaks, in English, to a room

They presented what they built and what they learned to an audience of adults. That kind of confidence doesn't come from a worksheet, and it doesn't wear off.

the boring stuff we take seriously

Small, supervised, seriously run

You're trusting us with your kid for a week of summer. Here's exactly how we've set it up.

🧑‍🏫

Max 10 kids. 4 trainers.

We cap every cohort at 10 children, with 4 trainers on the floor. That's roughly one adult for every 2 to 3 kids, so every founder gets real attention every day.

🔐

An adult is always at the keyboard

Kids never use AI tools alone. Every AI session happens on a shared screen with a trainer operating the tool: the kids think, decide and give the instructions; the adult types. Same rule for anything published online.

📷

Your kid's face stays private by default

Anything published online focuses on the projects, not the children. Kids only appear in photos or video with your explicit written consent, collected at registration.

frequently asked

Questions parents ask

Can we try it before committing?
Yes — the first day is free. Your child joins Monday's building afternoon at no cost, and you only confirm and pay for the rest of the week if they want to keep going. Spots are still capped at 10 kids, so we ask you to apply first so we can reserve one.
Where exactly does it happen?
The building afternoons happen at Productized HQ: R. de Campolide 28, 1070-072 Lisbon, in Amoreiras, right by the French School (Lycée Français). Drop-off from 13:50, pick-up at 18:00. The optional sports morning happens at Clube Nacional de Natação in São Bento (8:30–12:30); kids have lunch there and arrive at the afternoon session fed and ready.
Who is watching my child, and how closely?
Four trainers for a maximum of 10 kids, all afternoon, every day. That's roughly one adult per 2 to 3 children. Kids are never unsupervised, online or offline: every AI session and everything published to the internet happens with an adult at the keyboard. What about photos, video and privacy?
Kids shoot product photos and record a promotional podcast in our studio. Anything published online focuses on the projects; children only appear with explicit written consent from parents, collected at registration. It's always your call.
Is my 10-year-old too young for this? Is my 13-year-old too old?
Neither. The program is designed for ages 10 to 13, and teams are grouped by age and stage. Older founders take mentor roles like CEO or storyteller, younger ones get more hands-on trainer support, so a 10-year-old is never lost and a 13-year-old is never bored. Every project moves at its own team's pace.
What's included in the price, and what if plans change?
The price covers the full 5 building afternoons, all materials, pro camera and studio time, digital tools, the afternoon snack and Showcase Day. Kids bring their own laptop or tablet. Will the kids use AI on their own?
Always with an adult. Generative AI tools have minimum-age requirements, so they are always used together with an adult facilitator, on a shared screen or with the adult at the keyboard. The kids think, decide and give the instructions; the adult operates the tool.
My child doesn't speak English well. Is that a problem?
The program is taught in English, but our trainers are native speakers of English, Portuguese and French, so no kid ever gets stuck for language reasons. Most children pick up the working vocabulary within a day, and presenting in English on Friday is one of the proudest moments of the week.
What does putting a project online actually mean?
Each team publishes a simple project page (built with AI, adult at the keyboard) that family and friends can visit during the week. No child handles online payments or personal data.
What kind of projects can my kid work on?
Anything they're already into. A fantasy-league helper, a page for their gaming group, a player-stats tracker, a sneaker or card price tracker, a fan site for their favourite team. The interest comes from the kid; we bring the tools and structure to turn it into something real they can show on Friday.
Do they need to bring a laptop or materials?
We ask all kids to bring their own laptop or tablet. All other materials, cameras, studio time and digital tools are provided. Just come lunched and bring energy (afternoon snack is on us).
Is sport included?
Optionally, yes! We offer a sports morning with Clube Nacional de Natação in São Bento, from 8:30 to 12:30 with lunch included. Kids go straight from sports to the building afternoon. Prefer beach or family time? Mornings stay yours, and the core program runs 14:00–18:00.
