what they take home

Skills school doesn't teach yet

Beyond the product they created (and the money they earned!), every kid develops a product crafting sense: how to add value and build something they actually care about. All sessions taught in English, with native trainers in English, Portuguese, French and Russian on the floor.

🤖

Generative AI as a tool

Brainstorm, write, design and build landing pages with AI, always with an adult at the keyboard. Not magic: a tool, and they learn to be the boss of it.

🎤

Talking to real customers

Build a survey, run interviews, hear "no" and change course based on facts. Product validation, ages 8 to 12 edition.

📸

Marketing that ships

Shoot products with pro cameras, design assets in Canva, and record a podcast with video in our studio to promote what they built.

🛒

Selling online from day one

A live landing page on Monday, pre-orders on Tuesday, a real shop page by Wednesday. Online selling is the week's spine, not the finale.

💶

Money with a purpose

Set prices, count costs and margin, give change, track every sale on a hand-made dashboard. Math kids actually want to do.

🗣️

Pitching in English

Present the product, the numbers and the lessons learned to a room full of adults. Confidence you can't get from a worksheet.

frequently asked

Questions parents ask

Will the kids use AI on their own?
No. Generative AI tools have minimum-age requirements, so they are always used together with an adult facilitator, on a shared screen or with the adult at the keyboard. The kids think, decide and give the instructions; the adult operates the tool.
My child doesn't speak English well. Is that a problem?
The program is taught in English, but our trainers are native speakers of English, Portuguese, French and Russian, so no kid ever gets stuck for language reasons. Most children pick up the working vocabulary within a day, and pitching in English on Friday is one of the proudest moments of the week.
What does "selling online" actually mean for kids?
Each team publishes a simple shop page (built with AI, adult at the keyboard) where family and friends can place pre-orders during the week. Payments are symbolic and collected in person at market day. No child handles online payments or personal data.
Is the money real?
Yes, in small symbolic amounts. Families buy the products at market day and via pre-orders. The point is the learning: pricing, change, margin and the thrill of the first sale. Each team decides what to do with its earnings.
What about photos, video and privacy?
Kids shoot product photos and record a promotional podcast in our studio. Anything published online focuses on the products; children only appear with explicit written consent from parents, collected at registration.
Do they need to bring a laptop or materials?
We ask all kids to bring their own laptop or tablet. All other materials, cameras, studio time and digital tools are provided. Just come lunched and bring energy (afternoon snack is on us).
Is sport included?
Optionally, yes! We offer a sports morning with Clube Nacional de Natação in São Bento, from 8:30 to 12:30 with lunch included — kids go straight from sports to the business afternoon. Prefer beach or family time? Mornings stay yours, and the core program runs 14:00–17:30.
